Professor Michael A. Santoro studies how ethical and legal duties are made enforceable — through governance, institutional design, and accountability mechanisms that function at scale. His current work focuses on artificial intelligence: who is responsible when AI systems fail, how accountability can be structured upstream before deployment, and what democratic institutions must do to govern AI in the public interest.

Working at the cutting edge of AI accountability and management, his current work builds on decades of scholarship in governance, accountability, and human rights — fields where the gap between ethical principle and enforceable practice has long been his central concern.

His work is guided by a simple premise: ethical principles matter only insofar as they are transparent, equitable, and enforceable.

Consulting & Expert Witness

Professor Santoro has served as an expert witness in landmark corporate governance cases, helping design accountability frameworks that have shaped institutional reform. He has testified in federal cases involving Merck and Wells Fargo, and advises on AI governance, corporate ethics, and human rights compliance. Member of the New York Bar since 1982.
